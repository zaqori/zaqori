What is the Future Weight Simulator?

The Future Weight Simulator projects where your weight will land if you maintain your current calorie balance. Most people don't think about the long-term impact of even small daily calorie surpluses or deficits. Eating 200 calories more than you burn each day — a small handful of nuts, a sweetened coffee — leads to a 9 kg weight gain over 5 years if it goes unnoticed.

This tool makes that invisible math visible. By computing your Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE) and projecting it forward, you see exactly what your weight does in 1, 5, 10, or 20 years at the current trajectory.

The Energy Balance Equation

Weight change follows a simple principle: 1 pound of fat ≈ 3,500 calories. So a 500-calorie daily deficit produces about 1 pound of fat loss per week, and a 200-calorie surplus produces about a 9 kg gain over 5 years. The formula is:

Weight Change = (Calories In − Calories Out) × Time ÷ 7,700 cal per kg

How to Use This Simulator

  1. Enter your current weight, height, age, gender, and activity level.
  2. Set how many years you want to project forward.
  3. Click "Project My Weight" to see your trajectory assuming current habits.
  4. Use the Calorie Impact Simulator to model what changing your intake does to the curve.

Benefits of Visualizing Your Weight Future

"I'll lose it next month" becomes much harder to believe when you see that today's small surplus compounds into a 30 kg gain by age 60. Conversely, the same math shows that a small daily deficit today is the difference between a healthy weight and obesity by middle age.

The simulator helps you see weight as the long-term outcome it actually is — not a short-term event. Once you can see the trajectory, small changes feel much more meaningful.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does this assume I don't change anything?

Yes. The simulator assumes your current calorie balance remains constant. In reality, weight affects metabolism, habits shift over time, and life events intervene. Treat the projection as a baseline.

How accurate is the calorie math?

Calorie math is accurate at the population level. For individuals, variance comes from metabolic differences, muscle mass, and activity tracking error. The simulator gives you a reasonable estimate, not a guarantee.

What if my weight changes year to year?

Real life has weight fluctuations. The simulator shows the trajectory assuming current habits. Use the Calorie Impact Simulator to model what specific changes do to your weight over time.

Should I use kg or pounds?

This calculator uses kg. To convert from pounds, divide by 2.205. The chart and stats will all be in kg.

What's a healthy weight projection?

BMI between 18.5 and 24.9 is the standard healthy range. The simulator's BMI calculation helps you see whether your projected weight lands in that range or outside it.
