What is the Social Media Cost Simulator?

The Social Media Cost Simulator is one of the most eye-opening tools on ZAQORI. Most people underestimate their daily social media use by 50-100%. They say "an hour" when it's actually two, three, or more. And because the activity feels "free" (no money exchanged), the time cost stays invisible.

This tool makes the cumulative cost visible. By projecting daily scrolling into months and years, you see exactly how much time — and how much money, in terms of your hourly value — you've spent on apps that didn't make your life meaningfully better.

The Time Tax Math

Social media's cost is simple: it's your time multiplied by what that time could have produced.

Total Cost = Daily Hours × 365 × Years × Hourly Value

For someone spending 2 hours a day for 5 years with a $30/hour time value, that's $109,500 of time — and over 3,650 hours that could have been invested elsewhere.

How to Use This Simulator

  1. Use your phone's screen-time tracker to find your actual daily social media use.
  2. Enter that number and your honest hourly value.
  3. Set a time horizon and choose what you could have been doing instead.
  4. Click "Calculate The True Cost" to see the projection.

Benefits of Visualizing Social Media Cost

The numbers are sobering. Many users realize they have spent the equivalent of multiple years of their adult life on platforms that add little lasting value. But the simulator is not about guilt — it's about choice. Once you see the cost, you can make informed decisions about whether the trade-off is worth it.

Many ZAQORI users cut their social media time by 50-70% after running this simulation and reinvest the time in reading, exercise, learning, or side projects. Use the Reading Progress and Productivity Growth simulators to model what you could do with the recovered time.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I find my actual social media use?

Check your phone's screen-time settings (iOS: Settings > Screen Time; Android: Settings > Digital Wellbeing). Most people are surprised — actual use is often 2-3× what they estimate.

How do I value my time?

Take your annual income, divide by 2,000 working hours, and add the value of leisure time. A common rule: $25-50/hour for most professionals. Higher if you're a high earner.

Is all social media bad?

No. Genuine connection, learning, and professional networking are valuable. The simulator helps you see when passive consumption crowds out active engagement with your real life.

What's a healthy social media use?

Most experts suggest under 30-60 minutes per day of intentional use. Anything beyond is usually reactive scrolling that adds little value.

How can I cut back?

Remove apps from your phone, use website blockers during work hours, schedule "phone-free" times (mornings, meals, before bed), and replace the habit with something you value (reading, walking, etc.).
