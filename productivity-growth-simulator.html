What is the Productivity Growth Simulator?

The Productivity Growth Simulator models how consistent focus builds into a meaningful career. Most professionals are interrupted every 8-12 minutes in modern knowledge work. Recovering from an interruption takes 23 minutes. That means many knowledge workers are productive for only a fraction of their official work hours.

This tool helps you see the long-term difference between busy-work hours and deep-work hours. The same total hours invested differently produce radically different career outcomes.

Deep Work vs. Shallow Work

Cal Newport's research on deep work shows that the ability to focus without distraction is becoming both rare and valuable in the modern economy. The formula approximates output:

Output = Hours × Quality × (1 + Industry Growth)^Years

Quality captures whether the work is deep (high value) or shallow (low value). Industry growth captures external opportunity.

How to Use This Simulator

  1. Enter your daily focused work hours and rate your focus quality.
  2. Set the time horizon and your industry's growth rate.
  3. Click "Project My Productivity" to see your output trajectory and career multiplier.
  4. Run scenarios with different focus quality to see what deep work actually delivers.

Benefits of Visualizing Productivity

Most professionals underestimate the value of focus. They assume career success comes from working more hours, but the data suggests it comes from working differently — protecting focus time, batching shallow work, and creating artifacts of deep thought.

The simulator helps you see the compounding effect of consistent deep work. Five years of 3 hours/day at quality 7 produces dramatically more output than 5 years of 6 hours/day at quality 3 — even though the latter sounds more impressive on a timesheet.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is deep work?

Deep work is sustained, distraction-free concentration on a cognitively demanding task. It's the kind of work that produces new value: writing, coding, designing, analyzing, creating. It's different from shallow work like email and meetings.

How can I increase my deep work?

Schedule it, protect it, and eliminate distractions. Phone in another room, email off, single task. The Pomodoro technique, time blocking, and a quiet workspace all help.

What about industry growth?

Some industries grow faster than others. Tech, biotech, and renewable energy are growing fast; print media, retail, and some traditional industries are shrinking. The simulator accounts for this external factor.

Does this work for entrepreneurs?

Yes, perhaps even more so. Founders who maintain deep work on their most important problems — the "one thing" — dramatically outperform those who get pulled into reactive shallow work.

What about rest and recovery?

Deep work is sustainable when paired with adequate rest. The 4-hour daily deep work limit reflects cognitive limits; beyond that, quality drops sharply.
